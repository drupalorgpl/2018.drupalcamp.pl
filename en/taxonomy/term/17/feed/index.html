version of distro with customisations (e.g. theme, additional features or adjustments)</li> </ul><p>These are just a few examples of Enterprise sites we have been building: <a href="https://www.getopensocial.com/showcases/pachamama-alliance" target="_blank">Pachamama Alliance</a>, <a href="https://www.getopensocial.com/showcases/cbi" target="_blank">Connecting Business Initiative for United Nations</a>, <a href="https://www.getopensocial.com/showcases/globaldevhub" target="_blank">GlobalDevHub</a>, <a href="https://www.getopensocial.com/showcases/sdg-philanthropy" target="_blank">SDG Philanthropy</a>. All hosted on Platform.sh. In April 2017 we launched our SaaS Basic version which allowed everyone to create an Open Social instance within just 10 minutes.</p> <p>The solution we needed to find had some requirements:</p> <ul><li>Freedom of choice regarding hosting location (Europe/USA/Australia/etc)</li> <li>It needs to work well for our developers without the need of extensive hosting knowledge</li> </ul><p><strong>In this session you'll learn:</strong></p> <ul><li>A bit about maintain multiple product variations (distro, saas, enterprise)</li> <li>How we leveraged Platform.sh to build a scalable SaaS Business</li> <li>How to maintain a Drupal (distro) SaaS collection of sites with with open-source tools</li> <li>The challenges we faced or still facing</li> <li>How to build your own SaaS infrastructure</li> </ul><p>This session is aimed at intermediate devops or developers interested in maintaining multiple Drupal sites as SaaS or if you are just interested in different ways to
